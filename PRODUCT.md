# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Internal staff and members of an association or small organization who manage
contact lists and send newsletters to their own audiences. They are comfortable,
recurring users rather than daily campaign professionals: they log in to maintain
address books and contacts, compose an email, preview it, and send to a list.
Their context is administrative work, not high-volume marketing. Mistakes (sending
the wrong content, sending to the wrong list, sending twice) are costly and
irreversible, so confidence at the moment of sending matters more than raw speed.
The interface is French-language ("Carnets et liste de diffusion").

## Product Purpose

A focused admin tool for managing address books, contacts, senders, and email
campaigns, then sending those campaigns to mailing lists. It exists so a small
team can run their own diffusion lists without a heavy, ad-driven marketing
platform. Success looks like a user confidently composing an email, previewing
exactly what recipients will receive, choosing the right list, and sending once,
without second-guessing or accidental duplicate sends.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, calm, clear. The voice is reassuring and unhurried, never flashy.
The tool should feel like dependable infrastructure: it states what will happen,
shows the consequences before they are committed, and gets out of the way. No
hype, no urgency theatrics, no decorative noise.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard.** No hero-metric template (big number + label +
  gradient accent), no identical icon-heading-text card grids, no decorative
  gradients standing in for hierarchy.
- **Loud consumer marketing email tools** (Mailchimp-style). Avoid busy, highly
  colorful, ad-heavy, mascot-driven interfaces that prioritize personality over
  clarity.
- **Cold enterprise admin back-office.** Avoid sterile, intimidating, all-gray
  dense screens that feel punishing to non-technical users.

## Design Principles

- **Show the consequence before the commit.** Sending is irreversible. Every
  send path surfaces a faithful preview, the target list, and recipient count
  before the action is confirmed. Destructive or one-way actions are always
  explicit and reversible where possible.
- **Calm by default, clear under pressure.** Quiet visual baseline with no
  decorative noise, so the few moments that matter (sending, deleting, importing)
  can use color and emphasis to draw the eye without competing with everything else.
- **One obvious next step.** On any screen the primary task is unambiguous.
  Reduce the chance of acting on the wrong record, the wrong list, or the wrong button.
- **Plain language over jargon.** French labels and messages are concrete and
  human: say what an action does and what its result will be, not abstract system terms.
- **Earn trust through consistency.** Predictable layout, predictable wording,
  predictable behavior across resources, so recurring users build muscle memory
  and never feel surprised.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain sufficient text and UI contrast, full keyboard
navigation with visible focus states, and respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Do
not rely on color alone to convey state (sent, failed, pending): pair color with
text or icon. Keep tap and click targets comfortable for less tech-confident users.
